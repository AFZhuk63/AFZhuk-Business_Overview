
# Anatoliy Zhuk

# Biography

Software engineer, systems architect, entrepreneur, engineering executive, and technology founder whose professional career spans more than four decades across engineering, industrial construction, international trade, commodity exchange development, investment management, energy infrastructure, and enterprise software engineering.

Throughout his career, he has participated in the creation and management of engineering organizations, commodity exchanges, petroleum trading companies, construction enterprises, international investment projects, and technology initiatives. His professional experience combines systems engineering, executive leadership, strategic planning, investment analysis, international business development, and software architecture.

During different stages of his career he managed organizations operating in industrial engineering, international trade, petroleum products, gold mining, publishing, construction, and digital technologies. This multidisciplinary background later became the foundation for designing complex distributed software systems.

Since 2025, his primary professional focus has been the development of **Hippocrates Global** — an AI-ready healthcare platform designed as a secure, cloud-native ecosystem for modern medical services.

The project represents the synthesis of more than forty years of engineering practice, executive management, infrastructure development, and software architecture.

This biography summarizes documented milestones of his education, engineering practice, executive leadership, investment activities, software engineering, and ongoing technology research.

## Participation in Major Infrastructure Projects

Alongside the development of international investment initiatives, Globus LLC expanded its activities into 
large-scale transport and civil infrastructure construction.

During this period, the company participated in a number of strategically important engineering projects in 
Moscow and the Moscow Region, significantly strengthening its expertise in reinforced concrete structures, 
transport infrastructure, construction management, and multidisciplinary engineering coordination.

One of the company's most significant publicly documented achievements occurred in 2011, when the 
relatively young engineering and construction company **Globus LLC** reached the final stage of the 
government tender for the reconstruction of **Warsaw Highway**, one of Moscow's largest transport 
infrastructure projects with a contract value of up to **RUB 11.7 billion**.

The competition brought together several of Russia's leading infrastructure contractors, including:

- **Ingeokom**, founded by **Mikhail Rudyak**;
- **ARKS**, one of Russia's largest transport infrastructure companies, which later became part of the 
business group controlled by structures associated with **Gennady Timchenko**;
- other major federal construction contractors.

Following the initial commercial evaluation, **Globus LLC submitted the lowest financial proposal — RUB 10.7 billion — outperforming the nearest competitor by only RUB 8**.

Compared with the initial contract value of RUB 11.7 billion, the company's RUB 10.7 billion proposal represented potential savings of approximately **RUB 1 billion** for the Moscow city budget. 

Although the final government contract was ultimately awarded to another participant, the tender 
demonstrated that a relatively young independent engineering company led by **Anatoliy Zhuk** was capable 
of competing successfully with the country's largest infrastructure contractors on projects of national 
importance.

The company's participation in this competition became one of the most notable milestones in its 
engineering history and received coverage in leading Russian business media.

# Founder and Solution Architect

## Hippocrates Global

**2025–Present**

In 2025, **Anatoliy Zhuk** founded **Hippocrates Global**, an international technology initiative focused on the development 
of a next-generation healthcare ecosystem.

Rather than creating another electronic medical record system, the objective was to design an extensible engineering 
platform capable of integrating clinical workflows, telemedicine, artificial intelligence, medical robotics, 
rehabilitation technologies, and future human-machine interfaces within a unified architecture.

From its inception, the platform was conceived according to engineering principles commonly applied in aerospace, 
industrial automation, and mission-critical infrastructure:

- modular architecture;
- strict separation of responsibilities;
- independent subsystems;
- scalability by design;
- security by architecture;
- comprehensive documentation;
- long-term maintainability;
- cloud-native deployment.

The project is being developed as a multi-tenant enterprise platform capable of serving healthcare providers ranging 
from individual clinics to national healthcare systems.

---

# Platform Architecture

The **Hippocrates Global** platform is organized as a collection of independent but interconnected engineering modules.

Core architectural domains include:

- identity management;
- patient management;
- physician management;
- clinic administration;
- scheduling;
- appointment management;
- electronic medical records;
- configurable medical documentation;
- laboratory integration;
- billing;
- notifications;
- investor portal;
- administrative services.

The platform adopts a document-driven architecture in which medical forms, clinical documentation, and regulatory 
standards are represented as configurable engineering assets rather than hard-coded software components.

This approach enables rapid adaptation to different healthcare systems while preserving architectural consistency 
and maintainability.
